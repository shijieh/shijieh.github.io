## 🌊 About Me
I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of Hawai'i at Manoa[cite: 15]. My research is dedicated to supporting informed coastal management, enhancing community resilience, and advancing sustainable energy solutions. 

My expertise lies in **wave energy conversion**, **nearshore hydrodynamics**, **reef-wave interactions**, and **coastal sediment transport**. I specialize in utilizing **Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)** and high-performance computing to solve complex coastal engineering challenges.

---

## 🔬 Research Interests & Impact

### Wave Energy Conversion
* Developed a novel numerical method to efficiently model power takeoff devices (turbines).
* This method significantly reduces simulation time and has been **adopted by the American Bureau of Shipping (ABS)**.
* Investigated wave loading and vortex patterns around Oscillating Water Column (OWC) foundations to mitigate bed scour.

### Coastal Resilience & Coral Reefs
* Developed a validated 3D numerical model using **OpenFOAM** to simulate hydrodynamics in idealized barrier reef systems.
* Identified key coastal erosion mechanisms and proposed nature-based solutions, including coral reef restoration and artificial reef crests.
* Currently collaborating with the **Hawaii Department of Transportation (HDOT)** to predict long-term shoreline evolution for hazard mitigation.

### Advanced Sediment Transport Modeling
* Developing two-way coupling sediment transport models that capture dynamic interactions between sediment movement and hydrodynamics.
* This approach enhances prediction accuracy over traditional one-way coupling methods.
